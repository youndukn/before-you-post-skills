# Writing Profile Initialization Example

## Prompt

```text
Use the linkedin-founder-post-review skill to initialize my writing profile.

Audience: solo founders, technical founders, and early-stage builders
Voice: direct, practical, personal when useful
Avoid: hype, generic motivation, algorithm talk

Past post 1:
I used to think building was the hard part.

Now I think the hard part is knowing what not to build.

Every week there is a new tool that makes shipping faster.
But shipping faster only helps if the thing is pointed at a real problem.

Past post 2:
The best product feedback I got this week was silence.

I showed someone the idea.
They said it was interesting.
Then they did nothing.

That told me more than the compliment.

Past post 3:
I do not want to build another AI wrapper.

I want to build something where AI gets you to the draft,
but another human tells you where trust breaks.
```

## Expected Output

```markdown
## Founder Writing Profile

### Audience
Solo founders and technical builders who care about practical validation.

### Topics
- Build speed vs demand.
- Founder validation.
- Trust and product risk.
- AI as leverage, not the whole product.

### Voice
- Direct.
- Reflective but not sentimental.
- Short paragraphs.
- Uses simple contrasts.

### Structure Patterns
- Typical hook: "I used to think X. Now I think Y."
- Paragraph rhythm: one idea per short paragraph.
- Evidence style: recent founder observations and product decisions.
- Ending style: clear takeaway, often without a heavy CTA.

### Avoid
- Growth-hack language.
- Over-polished LinkedIn phrasing.
- Generic motivation.
```
